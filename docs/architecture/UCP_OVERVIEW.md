# Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) Overview

**Repository:** [Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp)

## What is UCP?

The Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) is an open-source standard designed to facilitate seamless commerce interactions between consumers, businesses, and payment providers. Developed by Google in collaboration with major industry players (Shopify, Target, Walmart, Mastercard, Visa, Stripe, and others), UCP aims to unify and standardize digital commerce for the era of agentic commerce.

## Key Features

- **Interoperability:** Provides a standardized framework for communication between diverse e-commerce systems, eliminating the need for custom integrations.
- **Agentic Commerce:** Enables AI agents to autonomously discover products, manage carts, and complete purchases on behalf of users, streamlining the shopping process.
- **Flexible Integration:** Offers standardized APIs for businesses to customize checkout experiences while retaining control over business logic.
- **Security & Consent:** Incorporates advanced security measures, including cryptographic proof of user consent for every transaction.

## Benefits for Stakeholders

- **Businesses:** Enhanced product visibility, simplified onboarding to new platforms, and increased sales opportunities.
- **Consumers:** Seamless, personalized shopping experiences with reduced friction and faster transactions.
- **Developers:** Open-source, extensible standard that encourages innovation in the commerce ecosystem.

## Impact

UCP is expected to reshape the digital commerce landscape by promoting interoperability, reducing friction, and enabling new agent-driven shopping experiences. As adoption grows, it will make online shopping more efficient, secure, and user-friendly.
